# Vision

NoAudience exists because consuming media has become a performance. You finish a movie and the first instinct is to rate it publicly, post about it, share a take. You read a book and feel pressure to leave a review that other people will judge. Somewhere along the way, the act of tracking what you watch and read turned into content creation.

This project starts from one question: does every hobby need an audience?

The answer, for me, is no. I wanted a place to log what I watch, what I read, and what I think — without publishing it. A private library that looks and feels as good as Letterboxd and Goodreads, but exists only on my laptop. No accounts. No followers. No feeds. No one watching.

## What this is

A desktop app that combines the best parts of Letterboxd (for films), Goodreads (for books), Substack (for articles and writing), and a chess PGN viewer — stripped of every social feature and run locally.

You open it like you open a journal. There is no login screen because there are no users. There is only you.

## What this is not

- Not a social network
- Not a recommendation engine
- Not a cloud service
- Not trying to grow a user base or track engagement
- Not monetized

## Why open source

Because other people might want this too. The whole thing is modular — if someone only wants a private Letterboxd, they can use just the Films module. If someone only wants a private Goodreads, they can use just the Books module. Take what you need, ignore the rest.

## Why these specific apps as inspiration

Letterboxd and Goodreads have spent years refining how people interact with media tracking. The UX patterns they've landed on — the diary view, the shelf system, the poster grids, the star ratings — work well. There is no reason to redesign what already works. NoAudience clones the interaction patterns faithfully and removes the parts that require an audience.

Substack's editor is clean and distraction-free. Obsidian's live preview makes markdown editing feel natural. The writing module takes the best of both.

## The name

NoAudience. It says what it is.
